📚 About the GED Mathematical Reasoning test

The GED Math test has 46 questions and 115 minutes. It is divided into two parts:

  • Part 1 (~5 questions, ~10 min): NO calculator allowed. Tests number sense and basic arithmetic.
  • Part 2 (~41 questions, ~105 min): Calculator allowed (TI-30XS on screen). A formula sheet is also provided.

Passing score: 145 out of 200. You need approximately 60–65% correct to pass.

Four content areas
Basic Math (45%)
Number operations • Fractions • Decimals • Percentages • Ratios • Proportions • Basic geometry
Algebraic Thinking (55%)
Linear equations • Inequalities • Quadratics • Patterns • Functions • Graphs • Tables

⚠ What makes GED Math questions different

Most GED Math questions are presented as real-world word problems — budgets, distances, construction projects, shopping scenarios. You will not just be asked "solve 3x + 5 = 14." You will be asked a scenario like "A contractor charges $45 per hour plus a $75 fee. If a customer pays $255, how many hours did the job take?" You must set up the equation yourself from the scenario.

Very commonPercentages — three types⌄
Every percent problem is one of three types. Identify which type, then use the matching setup.
Type 1: What is X% of N? → multiply by decimal   (35% of 80 = 0.35 × 80 = 28)
Type 2: Y is what % of N? → divide   (21 is what % of 60? = 21 ÷ 60 = 0.35 = 35%)
Type 3: Y is X% of what? → divide by decimal   (24 is 30% of what? = 24 ÷ 0.30 = 80)
% change = (New − Old) ÷ Old × 100
Word problem: A jacket costs $120 and is on sale for 25% off. What is the sale price?
Discount = 0.25 × 120 = $30  →  Sale price = 120 − 30 = $90 ✓
Very commonRatios and proportions⌄
A ratio compares two quantities. A proportion sets two ratios equal to each other. Solve proportions by cross-multiplying.
a/b = c/d  →  cross multiply  →  a × d = b × c
Word problem: A car travels 150 miles on 5 gallons. How far can it go on 8 gallons?
150/5 = x/8  →  5x = 1200  →  x = 240 miles ✓
Very commonFractions, decimals & order of operations⌄
Order of operations (PEMDAS): Parentheses → Exponents → Multiply/Divide → Add/Subtract

Fractions: Add/subtract: find common denominator first. Multiply: top × top, bottom × bottom. Divide: flip the second fraction, then multiply.

Negative numbers: negative × negative = positive. negative × positive = negative.
3 + 4 × 2 − (5 − 1)²
= 3 + 4 × 2 − 4² = 3 + 8 − 16 = −5 ✓
CommonExponents and square roots⌄
x² means x × x    x³ means x × x × x
x&sup0; = 1 (anything to the 0 power = 1)
x−² = 1/x²
√(x × y) = √x × √y
Perfect squares to know: 1,4,9,16,25,36,49,64,81,100,121,144
Simplify: √75
√75 = √(25 × 3) = 5√3 ✓
CommonMean, median, mode & range⌄
Mean = sum of all values ÷ number of values
Median = middle value when sorted (avg of two middle if even count)
Mode = value appearing most often
Range = largest − smallest
Data: 4, 7, 7, 9, 13
Mean = 40 ÷ 5 = 8   Median = 7   Mode = 7   Range = 9
Most testedSolving linear equations (word problems)⌄
Most GED algebra questions start with a scenario. Your job is to:
1. Read carefully — identify what is unknown (let x = ?)
2. Translate the words into an equation
3. Solve for x
4. Check that your answer makes sense in context
Word problem: A plumber charges $60 per hour plus a $40 trip fee. A customer pays $220. How many hours did the plumber work?
Let h = hours worked
60h + 40 = 220
60h = 180
h = 3 hours ✓
Very commonSlope and linear equations⌄
Slope m = (y&sub2; − y&sub1;) ÷ (x&sub2; − x&sub1;) = rise ÷ run
Slope-intercept form: y = mx + b (m = slope, b = y-intercept)
Positive slope ↗   Negative slope ↘   Zero slope → (flat)   Undefined slope ↑ (vertical)
Find slope through (1, 3) and (4, 9):
(9 − 3) ÷ (4 − 1) = 6 ÷ 3 = 2 ✓
Word problem: A phone plan charges $30/month plus $0.10 per text. Write an equation for the monthly cost C if you send t texts.
C = 0.10t + 30 (this is y = mx + b form) ✓
Very commonInequalities⌄
Solve like an equation — except: when you multiply or divide by a negative number, FLIP the inequality sign.
< means less than   > means greater than
≤ means less than OR equal to   ≥ means greater than OR equal to
Solve: −3x + 6 > 15
−3x > 9  →  divide by −3, FLIP: x < −3 ✓
Word problem: You can spend at most $200 on supplies. Each box costs $12. How many boxes can you buy?
12b ≤ 200  →  b ≤ 16.67  →  at most 16 boxes ✓
CommonSystems of equations⌄
Two equations, two unknowns. Use substitution (plug one equation into the other) or elimination (add/subtract equations to cancel one variable).
Adult tickets cost $8, child tickets cost $5. 10 tickets were sold for $62. How many of each?
a + c = 10   and   8a + 5c = 62
From equation 1: a = 10 − c
Substitute: 8(10−c) + 5c = 62 → 80 − 8c + 5c = 62 → −3c = −18 → c = 6
a = 10 − 6 = 4 adults, 6 children ✓
CommonQuadratic equations⌄
Standard form: ax² + bx + c = 0
Quadratic formula: x = (−b ± √(b²−4ac)) ÷ 2a
Factoring: find two numbers that multiply to c and add to b
Solve by factoring: x² + 5x + 6 = 0
Find factors of 6 that add to 5: 2 and 3
(x + 2)(x + 3) = 0 → x = −2 or x = −3 ✓
CommonFunctions and patterns⌄
A function assigns exactly one output (y) to each input (x). Written as f(x).

To evaluate a function, substitute the given value for x and calculate.
If f(x) = 3x² − 2x + 1, find f(4).
f(4) = 3(16) − 2(4) + 1 = 48 − 8 + 1 = 41 ✓
Pattern: 3, 7, 11, 15 ... what is the 10th term?
Each term adds 4. Formula: 3 + 4(n−1). For n=10: 3 + 36 = 39 ✓

Very commonArea, perimeter & volume⌄
Rectangle: Area = l × w    Perimeter = 2l + 2w
Triangle: Area = ½ × b × h
Circle: Area = πr²    Circumference = 2πr    (π ≈ 3.14)
Rectangular box (prism): Volume = l × w × h
Cylinder: Volume = πr²h
Word problem: A circular swimming pool has a diameter of 18 feet. What is the area of the pool? (Use π = 3.14)
Radius = 18 ÷ 2 = 9 ft
Area = 3.14 × 9² = 3.14 × 81 = 254.34 sq ft ✓
Very commonPythagorean theorem⌄
a² + b² = c²   (right triangles only)
c = hypotenuse (the longest side, opposite the right angle)
Common triples: 3-4-5    5-12-13    8-15-17
Word problem: A ladder 10 feet long leans against a wall. The base is 6 feet from the wall. How high up the wall does the ladder reach?
6² + h² = 10² → 36 + h² = 100 → h² = 64 → h = 8 feet ✓
CommonCoordinate geometry⌄
Distance between two points: d = √((x&sub2;−x&sub1;)² + (y&sub2;−y&sub1;)²)
Midpoint: ((x&sub1;+x&sub2;)÷2, (y&sub1;+y&sub2;)÷2)
Find the distance between (1, 2) and (4, 6).
d = √((4−1)² + (6−2)²) = √(9 + 16) = √25 = 5 ✓
CommonAngles and triangles⌄
Triangle angle sum: All 3 angles of any triangle add up to 180°
Straight line: Angles on a straight line add up to 180°
Supplementary angles: Two angles that add to 180°
Complementary angles: Two angles that add to 90°
Vertical angles: Opposite angles formed by two crossing lines are equal
A triangle has angles of 45° and 70°. Find the third angle.
180 − 45 − 70 = 65° ✓
Very commonReading and interpreting graphs & tables⌄
Many GED math questions give you a graph or table and ask you to extract information, identify trends, or calculate values.

Always check first: What do the axes or columns represent? What are the units? What time period is shown?

Bar graphs: Compare values between categories. Read height of bar from y-axis.
Line graphs: Show change over time. Steeper line = faster rate of change.
Scatter plots: Look for trend (positive, negative, or no correlation).
Tables: Find the row and column intersection carefully.
Very commonProbability⌄
Probability = favorable outcomes ÷ total possible outcomes
Always between 0 (impossible) and 1 (certain)
P(not A) = 1 − P(A)
A bag has 3 red, 5 blue, and 2 green marbles. What is the probability of picking a red marble?
P(red) = 3 ÷ (3+5+2) = 3/10 = 30% ✓
CommonInterpreting linear models⌄
Many GED questions give you a table of values or a graph and ask you to write or interpret a linear equation.

Step 1: Find the slope (rate of change) from two points in the table.
Step 2: Find the y-intercept (the starting value when x = 0).
Step 3: Write the equation in y = mx + b form.
Step 4: Use the equation to predict values not in the table.
A table shows hours worked (x) and pay (y): (1, 18), (2, 26), (3, 34). Write a linear equation.
Slope = (26−18)÷(2−1) = 8. When x=0: y = 18−8 = 10.
Equation: y = 8x + 10 (hourly rate $8, base fee $10) ✓

🎯 The most important skill: translating word problems

Most GED math questions are word problems. The math itself is usually not hard — the challenge is reading the problem carefully and setting it up correctly. Practice pulling key numbers out and deciding what equation to write before you start calculating.

4-step approach for every word problem
  1. Read the whole problem once — understand the scenario before touching any numbers.
  2. Identify what you need to find — write "let x = ___" to name the unknown.
  3. Set up the equation or calculation — translate the words into math.
  4. Solve, then check — does the answer make sense in the context of the problem?
Key word translations
is / equals / was / will be  →  =
sum / total / plus / added to / more than  →  +
difference / less than / fewer / decreased by  →  −
product / times / of / multiplied by  →  ×
quotient / divided by / per / ratio of  →  ÷
at least  →  ≥    no more than / at most  →  ≤
exceeds / more than  →  >    fewer than / less than  →  <
Part 1 — No calculator preparation

⚠ The first 5 questions have NO calculator

Many students are caught off guard by Part 1. Practice these skills without any calculator: multiplication tables (up to 12×12) • Dividing fractions • Finding 10%, 25%, and 50% mentally • Adding and subtracting negative numbers • Estimating answers by rounding

Time management — 115 minutes for 46 questions
You have about 2.5 minutes per question on average.
If a question is taking more than 3 minutes — make your best guess and move on. Come back if time allows.
Don't spend 10 minutes on one question while skipping five easy ones.
Word problems often take longer — budget extra time for multi-step scenarios.
Always eliminate obviously wrong answers first, even when guessing.
