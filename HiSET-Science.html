Science Strategies

The HiSET Science test is about reading and reasoning — not just memorizing facts.

💡 Test-Taking Strategies (all questions)
Read the stimulus before the question

Every question has a graph, table, or experiment description. Before reading the question, scan the stimulus: read the title, axis labels, column headers, and units. This builds context so the question makes sense immediately.

Know the difference: Hypothesis vs. Data vs. Conclusion
  • Hypothesis: a testable prediction made BEFORE the experiment
  • Data / Observation: what was actually measured or seen during the experiment
  • Conclusion: what the data shows AFTER the experiment — must be supported by evidence
  • Assumption: something taken for granted that was not directly tested
Eliminate wrong answers using the stimulus
  • Any answer that goes BEYOND what the data shows is wrong
  • Any answer that contradicts the data is wrong
  • Watch for “always,” “never,” or “proves” — science rarely makes absolute claims
  • The correct answer is the most directly supported by the evidence given
Reading graphs quickly
  • Read the TITLE first — it tells you what the whole graph is about
  • Read axis labels and UNITS — always check what is being measured
  • Look at the trend: going up? going down? leveling off? no pattern?
  • For line graphs: steep slope = fast change; flat = no change
🥎 I. Life Science — 49% of the test
Ecosystems and food webs
  • Producers (plants) make their own food via photosynthesis — base of every food chain
  • Energy flows: sun → producers → primary consumers → secondary consumers
  • Energy is lost at each level — producers have the most energy in the system
  • If one species declines, trace the direct effect up and down the chain
Cell biology key facts
  • Mitochondria: produces energy (ATP) — found in all cells
  • Chloroplasts: photosynthesis — plant cells ONLY
  • Nucleus: contains DNA, controls cell
  • Cell wall: plant cells only; animal cells have only a cell membrane
  • Ribosomes: make proteins
DNA and genetics
  • DNA carries genetic information; genes = segments of DNA
  • Mitosis = growth/repair (identical cells); Meiosis = sex cells (half chromosomes)
  • Dominant allele (capital) masks recessive (lowercase); only tt shows recessive trait
  • Punnett square: Tt × Tt gives 25% TT, 50% Tt, 25% tt → 75% show dominant trait
Photosynthesis vs. Cellular Respiration
  • Photosynthesis: CO² + H²O + sunlight → glucose + O² (in chloroplasts)
  • Cellular respiration: glucose + O² → CO² + H²O + energy (in mitochondria)
  • These two processes are essentially opposites of each other
⚛ II. Physical Science — 28% of the test
Newton’s Three Laws
  • 1st Law (Inertia): objects stay at rest or in motion unless a force acts on them
  • 2nd Law: F = ma — friction causes deceleration; more mass needs more force
  • 3rd Law: every action has an equal and opposite reaction
Energy types and conversion
  • Potential energy (PE): stored, due to position or condition
  • Kinetic energy (KE): energy of motion
  • Going downhill: PE decreases, KE increases (total stays constant)
  • Energy is conserved — never created or destroyed, only converted
Atoms and chemical reactions
  • Atomic number = protons; Neutrons = mass number − atomic number
  • Chemical change = new substance formed (burning, rusting, cooking)
  • Physical change = same substance, different form (melting, dissolving)
  • pH: below 7 = acidic; 7 = neutral; above 7 = basic. Bases raise pH; acids lower it.
🌍 III. Earth Science — 23% of the test
Rock types — three to know
  • Igneous: cooled from magma or lava (no fossils, crystalline)
  • Sedimentary: compressed layers of sediment (may contain fossils)
  • Metamorphic: existing rock changed by heat and pressure (recrystallized)
Plate tectonics — three boundary types
  • Convergent: plates push together → mountains or ocean trenches
  • Divergent: plates pull apart → mid-ocean ridges
  • Transform: plates slide past each other → earthquakes (e.g. San Andreas Fault)
Earth’s layers and cycles
  • Layers outside to inside: Crust → Mantle → Outer Core → Inner Core
  • Water cycle: evaporation → condensation → precipitation → runoff
  • Seasons: caused by Earth’s axial tilt, NOT distance from the Sun
  • Planet order: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune
